Japan's Foods

Sashimi is a delicious traditional Japanese dish, in which the main ingredients are fresh seafood. The seafood used to make Sashimi is caught using separate tools, then processed under a special process to ensure the freshness of each piece of sashimi. Some types of seafood that the Japanese often use as sashimi are: Salmon, tuna, mackerel, basa, puffer fish, squid, shrimp, octopus...

France's Foods

Foie gras is a scrumptious dish of French cuisine, available in most international standard restaurants and moreover not all restaurants are capable of processing it. Foie gras is prepared from geese that are cared for with a special diet to harness the nutritional content of their liver. The way to prepare this dish is also considered the most amazing feat of all.

Singapro's Foods

Chili Crab was invented by a Singaporean chef in 1950. Since then, this dish seems to have become the "national drug" of this country. The crab is mixed with hot egg tomato sauce accompanied by the spicy taste of chili that will surely make any guest fall in love with even the most gourmet.

ThaiLan's Foods

Tom Yum is a Thai dish that is considered a famous specialty of Thailand, not only that in 2011 this dish was also voted and ranked 8th in the top 50 best dishes in the world voted by CNN. Made from shrimp and squid as the main ingredients, accompanied by the pungent taste of chili, lemongrass, and a little sourness of lemon. This is a dish that you should not miss when coming to Thailand.

China's Foods

Peking duck is a famous specialty dish in Northeast China, especially in Beijing. The specialty of roast duck is thin, crispy, dark yellow duck skin, many restaurants serve their own skin and meat dishes. Big and fat Peking duck after being roasted in a large fire oven, the restaurant cuts the meat and skin to serve customers, the remaining bones are stewed to cook soup.

American's Foods

South American ribs are fragrant and extremely stimulating to the taste buds. The ribs will be grilled with a large block in the oven or microwave, and then served with sauce or eaten, it's up to you. Try to hold a piece of ribs and eat comfortably to feel its delicious taste!
